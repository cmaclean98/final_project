Where is parking occupying potential housing development?

It is a uncontested truth that land within the five boroughs of NYC is precious.

However, how much of this land is dedicated to parking lots? According to the New York City Economic Development Corporation, only 22% of New Yorkers own cars. And yet, according to Too Many Cars NYC, there are 5,375,612 parking spaces across the five boroughs. That's over 480,000,000 square feet of just curbside parking based on the standard 9x18 spot size.

With vast improvements and access to public transit and a growing housing crisis, the city must prioritize housing development. As public officials consider strategies to meet growing housing demand through a variety of housing typologies and funding sources, it is imperative to examine the areas that contain an exhorbinant amount of parking. This map presents a case study of a few blocks of Manhattan's central business district, bound by Pearl Street to the East, William Street to the West, Fulton Street to the North, and Maiden Lane to the South. According to NYC DCP street widths, the case study zone represents roughly 4500 square feet. In the spirit of the Peterson Rich Office 9x18 project, just 2 parking spaces can equal a micro unit and 2.5 can equate to a 1-bedroom apartment. There is ample opportunity to build housing in this central business hub. The area is transit-rich-situated directly adjacent to a plethora of subway stops (namely Wall Street, Fulton Street Broad Street, and Cortlandt), bus routes, and Citi Bike stations.
